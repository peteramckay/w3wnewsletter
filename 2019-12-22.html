Week in Review: Dec. 15-21, 2019

Happy holidays! I'm doing my best Bob Cratchit impersonation today, taking a brief break from gingerbread cookies, egg nog, and whatnot to do a little cold, hard number-crunching.

This is all in the spirit of the year-end wrap up stories that would inevitably be our focus every December back when I covered Wall Street. These always got a lot of attention for two reasons:

(a) Since many finance pros were on vacation, there wasn't much other news being made anyway. And...

(b) The year-end naturally produced datapoints that neatly illustrated market trends, vindicated certain people's earlier predictions, and refuted others, all over the very user-friendly timeframe of a calendar year.

I figured we should do the same for the crypto market of 2019. Here are some of the year's notable token-related factoids:

  • The global market cap for all cryptoassets is now over $191 billion, up 52 percent in 2019. That's quite a 12-month recovery indeed from the "crypto winter" that many folks were fretting about coming into 2019. However, global market cap is still 77 percent below its all-time high of $815 billion set in January 2018, according to CoinMarketCap data.
  • The crypto market's 2019 gain of 52 percent handily beat the U.S. stock market, where the S&P 500 is up 28 percent.
  • Relative to some individual large-cap companies, the crypto market is now slightly bigger than the valuation of Boeing ($184 billion market cap), just shy of Pepsi ($192 billion), and distantly behind Apple's market-leading valuation ($1.3 trillion), according to Yahoo Finance data.
  • Of course, it's also worth comparing crypto to fiat currency for a moment. You know, for all the maximalists out there proclaiming the inevitability that bitcoin or other tokens will replace the U.S. dollar as the world's reserve currency. Here goes...
  • Daily trading volume in all cryptoassets recently hovered around $60 million (with an m) per day, down from a peak around $135 million (with an m) in June, according to CoinMarketCap. That compares to $6.6 trillion (with a t) per day in foreign exchange trading of fiat currencies like USD, according to the Bank of International Settlements.
  • Despite some recent market turbulence for bitcoin, it's had a strong year, up 97 percent in 2019. It's now trading just shy of $7,400, good for a market cap of $133 billion, which is of course the largest of any single token.
  • Coming into 2019, Bitcoin represented about half the total market cap of all cryptoassets. That proportion -- known among traders as "BTC dominance" -- crept higher through summer and has hovered around 70 percent since.
  • Aside from BTC, the top five tokens by market cap are ether ($14.3 billion), Ripple ($8.4 billion), Tether ($4.1 billion), Bitcoin Cash ($3.5 billion), and Litecoin ($2.7 billion).

In all, the numbers strike me as showing a lot of promise for crypto heading into the New Year. But at the same time, they underscore how much room there is for growth among the masses.

As ever, it's worth remembering that the blockchain space is still a fairly small niche in the scheme of things. Attaining wider appeal is a big challenge in terms of both product development and communication.
