Kubernetes is eating the world (in a good way)

The ecosystem around Kubernetes, an open-source system for managing virtual app containers, boomed in 2017, writes TechCrunch in a year-end look back.

This is one of those things that sounds deep in the weeds, but is really worth watching if you're in any industry that's being deeply disrupted by technology. (Which is all of them, basically.) The idea behind containerization -- an increasingly popular idea among IT folks -- is that it enables organizations to be more nimble in deploying new technology, and thus innovating at whatever their core mission is.

The idea has its critics, for sure. But in a world where speed is increasingly of the essence, containerization looks like its going to maintain a lot of momentum going into the New Year.

The problem: Traditional news broadcasters may soon have to pay additional fees to distribute their journalism online.
