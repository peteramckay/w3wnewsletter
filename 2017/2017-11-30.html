Recent independence-related info from other sites around the web:

  • Connecticut's attorney general is investigating recent revelations reported by Quartz that Google has been collecting location data from some Android phones without authorization. The collection went on even when users turned off location services in their phones' settings, according to Quartz.

  • Vice Media's tech site Motherboard has published a comprehensive, layman-friendly guide to not getting hacked.

  • Harvard's Nieman Lab reports that ad-supported digital media are demonstrably -- and increasingly -- being squeezed by the ad duopoly of Google and Facebook. Buzzfeed and Vice are going to miss thier quarterly revenue projections, Oath is conducting layoffs, and Mashable's recently sold itself at what amounts to a fire-sale valuation of $50 million. It had previously been worth $250 million.

  • The Poynter Institute's Daniel Funke tracked down the sources of specious anti-Muslim videos that President Trump recently retweeted. They've all been previously debunked. But, hey, why would that stop the leader of the free world from spreading the information anyway?
