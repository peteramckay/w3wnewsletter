Nov. 21-27, 2021


Even Thanksgiving in the US. was tokenized this year.

In the latest sign of blockchain's mainstreaming, the retailer Macy's is auctioning off a collection of 10 non-fungible tokens commemorating floats from each decade of its big Thanksgiving Day Parade in New York. The auction runs through Nov. 30, with proceeds going to the Make-A-Wish Foundation. So it's all for a great cause.  üèª

Unfortunately, the new float from this year's parade depicting Grogu (a.k.a. "Baby Yoda" to the uninitiated) is not among the ones being auctioned. But the addition was still nice to see, marking a milestone that I honestly found pretty surprising: Grogu was the first Star Wars character ever to have a float in the parade.

On to the week's other decentralization headlines:
  • What's going on at Parity Technologies? CoinDesk reports that co-founder and chief executive Jutta Steiner left the company "earlier this year" for undisclosed reasons. She's been replaced by fellow co-founder Gavin Wood. The move -- or at least the public confirmation of it -- comes as Parity's open-source Polkadot token has become the number-nine token in the world, with a valuation of $33.4 billion. Polkadot is designed to enable cross-chain compatibility of decentralized applications running designed for different networks -- a longtime wishlist items for developers. Polkadot is scheduled to activate the first group of projects to run on its new interoperable network on Dec. 17. 
  • Renewed COVID worries roiled asset markets, including crypto. Worries about the new Omicron variant of the virus roiled Wall Street's securities exchanges on Friday, with the selloff spilling into token prices as well as investors shied from risky assets in general. Bitcoin tumbled 8% for the week, recently trading near $55,000.
  • The chief financial officer of the crypto lending platform Celsius was arrested in Tel Aviv on charges of money laundering and fraud related to his former work for Israeli crypto mogul Moshe Hogeg, who faces similar charges, Celsius promptly tweeted a statement saying it had suspended the CFO and that no assets on its platform have been affected.  
  • Regal movie theaters are going to start accepting crypto payments for tickets and concessions, Regal is partnering with Flexa to implement the new payments, which effectively emulate a similar move by competitor AMC earlier this fall. That company's CEO recently said that crypto payments already account for 14% of AMC's online transactions with customers.
  • Capsule Social founder Nadim Kobeissi recently gave a layman-friendly talk in Bern, Switzerland, explaining why Apple's push to scan users' phones for child pornography set a precedent for user privacy that should concern everyone  who uses a smartphone.
  • The Washington Post reports that leaked documents suggest Facebook has been losing Black users since September 2020 due to lax policing of hate speech on the platform.
  • The Iron Sheikh agrees with crypto billionaire Tyler Winklevoss's investing advice. Not sure Tyler (or anyone else) really needed that assurance. But there we have it on Twitter anyway FYI.
