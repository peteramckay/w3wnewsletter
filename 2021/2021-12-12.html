Dec. 5-11, 2021


A must-watch television pick to kick things off this time:

Last week, 60 Minutes landed a blockbuster interview with whistleblower Reality Winner, who leaked secret information to alert the American public about Russia's efforts to undermine our elections in 2016. This is truly another of those touchstone sort of stories -- similar to previous ones about Edward Snowden and Chelsea Manning -- that underscore the serious risks democracy continues to face from mass surveillance and cyberattacks.

The question remains, however: Will we heed the warnings? Or will we continue to treat these whistleblowers as the real traitors, not the politicians and law enforcement people who insist on trampling the Bill of Rights?

The week's other notable headlines:
  • Grayscale Research released a comprehensive analysis of bitcoin investing trends this year. Among the findings: 55% of everyone who's invested in bitcoin globally got into it for the first time in 2021.
  • Microsoft and Warner Brothers were among the investors in Palm NFT Studio's latest funding round. The startup raised $27 million to expand its protocol, an energy-efficient Ethereum sidechain with low transaction costs to help non-fungible tokens reach more users.
  • Executives from major U.S. crypto exchanges got a mostly favorable reception at a House hearing on Wednesday. Members are still weighing new regulations on the industry, but many also expressed a desire not to stifle its potential for important innovation.
  • BitMart said it will compensate victims of a recent $196 million hack with its own funds.
  • WhatsApp launched a crypto payments pilot program in the U.S.
  • Speaking at the Wall Street Journal's CEO Summit, the director of the Central Intelligence Agency, William Burns, confirmed the is working on "a number of crypto projects." He provided few other details about the scope of the CIA's work, although fighting ransomware attacks seems to be one priority.
  • Joseph Delong, chief technology officer of the decentralized finance platform Sushi, resigned unexpectedly. The Defiant characterized the move as the latest installment in a widening "drama" around Sushi's leadership of late.
  • Former Google chief executive Eric Schmidt joined Chainlink Labs as an advisor. The company makes a protocol for sharing data across different platforms using smart contracts. Granted, you probably suspected that already. But there's the confirmation, for what it's worth.
  • An outage on the server platform Amazon Web Services on Tuesday disrupted many popular services, including Disney+, Netflix, Slack, and Coinbase.
  • Fast Company reports it will take more than a virtual office water cooler in the metaverse to fix the level of employee dissatisfaction fueling the Great Resignation.
