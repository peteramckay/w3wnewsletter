Nov. 28 to Dec. 4, 2021


The holiday season always has its share of stresses. Gift shopping. Travel delays. That one relative guaranteed to get on your nerves at the big family dinner...

And, oh yeah, sometimes the world's crypto markets suffer a hairy late-year crash. Which brings us to the week's headlines:
  • Token prices plunged around the world, led by bitcoin. The selling was led by institutional traders who are increasingly nervous about possible increases soon in central bank interest rates. Such moves would be aimed at fighting inflation but would also make leveraged speculation in crypto, stocks, and other assets more expensive.At its Friday lows, bitcoin was off 15%. But it recently trimmed its losses to trade just below $50,000, down 9% over the last 7 days. The global market capitalization of all crypto assets now stands at $2.3 trillion, down about 10% for the week.
  • Latin America's appetite for crypto remains undampened, however. El Salvador, which now recognizes bitcoin as legal tender, bought 100 more BTC tokens during the week's market dip. Meanwhile, legislators in Chile are weighing proposals to recognize bitcoin for payments and to offer a digital version of the country's peso.
  • Goldman Sachs is looking into potential ways to accept bitcoin as collateral for dollar-denominated loans.
  • Hacks everywhere. In a new report, Google said that a majority of recently attacked accounts on its Google Cloud Platform service were used to mine cryptocurrency. Meanwhile, BadgerDAO, a bitcoin-based decentralized finance project, was hacked for $120 million in assets. And the DeFI platform MonoX suffered a breach in which $31 million worth of several different tokens was stolen, including ether and polygon. 
  • Congress is set to question crypto exchange executives. A House committee will hear testimony Wednesday from executives at FTX, Coinbase, Paxos, and others about the opportunities and challenges around financial innovation in the U.S. Separately, Bullish a new EOS-based exchanged backed by famed Facebook investor Peter Thiel, launched trading services for institutional clients. And Cypto.com acquired two U.S. derivatives platforms for $216 million.
  • Mixed news from Meta on crypto. Facebook's parent company amended its policies to allow advertising from more digital-asset companies. But on a less auspicious note, David Marcus, co-creator of the Diem digital currency (formerly known as Libra) and current leader the Meta team building the Novi crypto wallet, announced Tuesday that he will leave the company at the end of the year.
  • Jack Dorsey stepped down as chief executive of Twitter. The move raised some crypto fans' hopes that Dorsey, who also leads Square and is a staunch bitcoiner, may expand his involvement in the blockchain sector. But he's announced no such specific plans so far.
  • Earth may someday have cool rings like Saturn's, according to researchers at the University of Utah. But there's bad news: The rings will be made of human-manufactured space junk.
