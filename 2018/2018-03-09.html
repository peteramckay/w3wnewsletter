Easing into DNDWG

I've been thinking about how to follow up on my recent blog post/rant about news delivery. This problem of Big Tech's lockdown on getting news to actual users has bugged me for a long time. But after getting it down in black and white, it's really, really burning a hole in my brain.

Then I realized: This is ripe turf for an open-source project. It cries out for collaboration across multiple news-industry entities all dealing with the same problem, as well as interested users, geeks, and so on.

Out of desperation, the news industry has gotten better at innovation the last 5-10 years, I think. But most of their innovation efforts still focus on newsgathering and storytelling -- the areas of most obvious interest for reporters and editors day-to-day.

That intuitively makes a lot of sense, of course. But I'm convinced we need to get much more assertive about working on the next piece of the news supply chain as well -- delivering the news to actual users.

In that spirit, I'm in the early stages of organizing a new open-source body: the Digital News Delivery Working Group. (The initials are DNDWG, which can be pronounced "downdog" for short, like the yoga pose :-)

I'm thinking DNDWG should focus on finding ways to deliver news to consumers that are:

  • Web-based
  • Publisher-hosted
  • Open source

If we take those three things as requirements, I think they dilineate the work to be done quite neatly. You'll quickly see, those three traits immediately cross off a lot of what currently passes as innovation in news delivery -- stuff like Facebook Pages (not publisher-hosted) or iDevice apps (not web-based).

While those tools may admittedly have a certain cool factor up front, they ultimately reinforce Big Tech's grip on the news, which is the real long-term problem for both the industry and society as a whole.

So let's set out to build different stuff via DNDWG -- tools that do precisely what Big Tech's tools never will.

This email is essentially a preview of DNDWG. I'm working on setting up a listserv and website for it, probably to launch in the next week or so. But I'm telling a few friends about it now, to get early feedback and ideas to refine the concept.

Please feel free to hit me back via email (peter@indizr.com) with any feedback. I've also already set up a DNDWG page on GitHub, which you can follow if you're on that network. There's also an open comment thread there if you want to post any ideas publicly.
