DNDWG listserv is now live

I've now set up a public email listserv for the Digital News Delivery Working Group. You can join by emailing dndwg-subscribe@yahoogroups.com, and post messages by emailing dndwg@yahoogroups.com.

When you join, you'll get a confirmation link to click from Yahoo Groups. You'll also need my approval as moderator to begn posting. That might sound like a lot, but it's basically a system of safeguards to keep spammers out. (You'll thank me later, believe me :-)

In practice, I think this setup should work fine. I also intend to approve everyone whose address looks legit, so the group is open to anyone interested. So you can definitely feel free now to pass along the signup link to other friends and colleagues.

As I said in an earlier newsletter, I want DNDWG to be a group for open collaboration to improve the delivery of news online. I want us to focus on solutions that are:

  • Publisher-Hosted: Stuff like Facebook Instant Articles, which enhances the flow of data moving across big Silicon Valley companies' servers, is decidely outside of scope here. If anything, it's precisely the harm to journalists from such sort of stuff that we're trying to mitigate.
  • Web-Based: Oh, right. The open web. Remember that? Let's get back to it.
  • Open Source: By avoiding hassle over proprietary licensing, we'll make it easier for users to adopt whatever we come up with widely and quickly.

I'm also going to add a web page and some in-person kick-off events shortly as well, at which point DNDWG will truly be up and running at full speed. But in the meantime, I'm trying to share resources with everyone as soon as I've set them up.

For now, please do feel free to start sharing ideas for news on the new DNDWG listserv. I hope we'll go back and forth over time between brainstorming and actually making cool new stuff.

On the Indizr blog, I recently shared what I believe might be a viable framework for getting news from publishers to consumers more directly. But I don't necessarily think that's the only way to do it. Curious to see what else everyone comes up with as well!
