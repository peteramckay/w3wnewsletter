The latest news about journalism innovation from around the web:

  • We were happy to hear this week from newspaper veteran Ron Roeder, who's working on a cool (and much-needed) experiment in local digital news. The aptly named site he co-founded, iGrewUpIn, asks users to identify their hometowns and then uses social platform-like features to connect them directly to local news sources.

    For now, iGrewUpIn is seeking feedback on its beta version. If you're game to try it out, you can test it as a demo user with the login name "demo" and password "demo1." After you've tried it out a bit, please email your thoughts to igrewupin@gmail.com. That would be a great help to get this project started on the right foot.

  • Writing for The Outline, Caroline Haskins says that Reddit may be the most crucial battleground right now in the broader fight to curtail trolling online.
  • Wired's Lauren Goode says tech companies have made it needlessly difficult to manage the data permissions on your smartphone's apps. Speaking of which...
  • Facebook's followup to Mark Zuckerberg's recent oh-so-earnest remarks to Congress about protecting users? To the claims it's open to new regulation? Move a massive batch of user data beyond reach of new privacy regulations taking effect in Europe. Of course.
  • Outgoing Gizmodo CEO Raju Narisetti responded to New York Magazine on Twitter this week with a useful reminder about how to frame discussions about new business models for news online.


Big Tech's platforms suck at news. Help us build better ones.

We've started an open-source project, the Digital News Delivery Working Group, to find better ways to get news to people using the web. (We call the group "downdog" for short, like the yoga pose :-) If you'd like to participate, the best way is to join the DNDWG email group by sending a message to dndwg-subscribe@yahoogroups.com. That group is our chief venue for brainstorming and collaborating. Please join us!
