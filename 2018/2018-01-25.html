Recent independence-related info from around the web:

  • DuckDuckGo, which offers anonymized web search, just released new versions of its browser plugin and mobile app to enhance other aspects of user privacy as well. For instance, the new tools will give the websites you visit privacy grades from A to F based on several factors, including their use of tracker codes.
  • Net neutrality remains in focus as non-federal actors scramble for workarounds to the recent rules change by the Federal Communications Commission favoring the telcom industry. At the state level, New York and Montana are using their leverage as customers on government contracts to pressure telcom providers into maintaining neutrality for everyone. And on the corporate front, Burger King has improbably weighed in on neutrality, using Whopper production as a metaphor to explain the issue. Turns out MBPS = "Making Burgers Per Second." Who knew?
  • Lawyers for the American Civil Liberties Union say they've been getting more calls from women being served defamation suits after making claims of sexual harrassment or abuse. The trend seems to be a quiet backlash against the #MeToo movement, the ACLU says.
  • Craig Forman, chief executive of the McClatchy newspaper chain, weighed in this week on the continued importance of local journalism in the U.S., even at a time when Donald Trump's presidency has dominated people's attention.
  • Rupert Murdoch, executive chairman of News Corp., issued a statement this week saying Facebook and Google ought to pay "carriage fees" to trusted news sources whose content they distribute on their platforms. Such a scheme would roughly approximate a longstanding practice in the television industry.
  • Disclosure #1: I used to work for Mr. Murdoch at the Wall Street Journal. One one occasion, I was unable to correctly direct him to the conference room where a metro staff meeting with Mayor Bloomberg was taking place.
  • Disclosure #2: For the record, I physically sat by the metro staff -- a new creation at the time -- but my beat was financial markets. You know, the shit the Journal is supposed to be covering.
  • Disclosure #3: I rather like this "carriage fees" idea.
