The latest news about journalism innovation from around the web:

  • The millennial-focused news site Mic is focusing less on Facebook these days, more on "deliliberate distribution" through Twitter, Apple News, and other platforms, Digiday reports.
  • Sharon Waxman, founder of entertainment news site The Wrap, issued an anti-Facebook tweetstorm for the ages, questioning the company's committment to truth and journalism. She also specified that her company won't participate in Facebook's new video initiative looking to partner with publishers to produce high-quality news segments. "I won't put lipstick on that pig," wrote Waxman, who previously covered Hollywood for the Washington Post.
  • The Nieman Lab blog has published a helpful guide to "the biggest brains and bank accounts in the fight for local journalism."
  • The Tow Center's Chi Zhang took an in-depth look at how misinformation spreads on WeChat. The service tends not to get much press attention compared to some of the bigger social platforms, but it's very popular among Chinese immigrants, Chi noted.
  • Oakland's Kapor Center had a great event over the weekend bringing together tech founders of color from around the Bay Area. If you missed it, you can still check out attendees' updates from the session at the hashtag #TECHxOAK.


Big Tech's platforms suck at news. Help us build better ones.

We've started an open-source project, the Digital News Delivery Working Group, to find better ways to get news to people using the web. (We call the group "downdog" for short, like the yoga pose :-) If you'd like to participate, the best way is to join the DNDWG email group by sending a message to dndwg-subscribe@yahoogroups.com. That group is our chief venue for brainstorming and collaborating. Please join us!
