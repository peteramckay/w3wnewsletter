Big Tech's platforms suck at news. Help us build better ones.

We've started an open-source project, the Digital News Delivery Working Group, to find better ways to get news to people using the web. (We call the group "downdog" for short, like the yoga pose :-) If you'd like to participate, the best way is to join the DNDWG email group by sending a message to dndwg-subscribe@yahoogroups.com. That group is our chief venue for brainstorming and collaborating. Please join us!



Recent independence-related info from around the web:

  • The Poynter Institute recently shared an update with some initial lessons from their Table Stakes project to help local publishers with digital adaptation.
  • Broadstreet founder Kenny Katzgrau weighed in on MediaShift about some unexpected advantages that small publishers might be able to leverage versus the likes of Google and Facebook in selling online ads.
  • Speaking of MediaShift, the 12-year-old publication's founder Mark Glaser announced it will end its weekly podcast and daily website updates covering digital news innovation. Instead, MediaShift will focus more time and energy on its paid training programs. The change is certainly understandable -- gotta make those dolla, dolla bills, y'all -- but we're more than a little bummed as big fans of MediaShift's content. Good luck, Mark!
  • Fight for the Future has put up a website specifically to rally conservatives in the U.S. to the cause of net neutrality. Hey, whatever works.
  • Southern Methodist University said it will take over its independent student newspaper in May. The move quickly drew the ire of free-press advocates and SMU alumni, including ProPublica politics reporter Jessica Huseman, who resigned as co-president of the university's New York alumni chapter. She wrote on Twitter that SMU "has no commitment at all to transparency and freedom of student voices."
  • Legendary CBS anchor Dan Rather took to Twitter recently to comment on the increasingly chaotic news cycle in the U.S. Rather wrote: "I love news. Have my entire life.. It's not only a profession, but a passion. Sometimes, however, even I feel I need a break. In an age of unrelenting headlines of dire global import, I've found an increasing joy in going for a walk. Preferably in the woods."
  • If software developer Brianna Wu weren't already running for Congress, we'd want her to after she tweeted this epic defense of the oft-forgotten Generation X.
  • Don't think we've ever done a book pick around here, but here's a timely one to pass along: Mary Shelley's Frankenstein. It's the story of a young, well-intended genius inventor who unleashes his creation on the public and tragically loses control of it. Sound familiar? You can download a free copy from Project Gutenberg.
