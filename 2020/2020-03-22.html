Web 3.0 Weekly

March 15-21, 2020

More than ever, now is the time to re-invent the web.

With more people working remote, keeping up with loved ones via apps, buying food, and generally living more of their lives online in lieu of in-person interaction, this is exactly the time to re-think the network and what we've flatly gotten wrong about it so far.

On the other side of the coronavirus crisis, let's have a web that does not exploit users, that respects privacy, that offers more economic choice rather than oligarchy, that is legitimately more innovative, more apt to tackle big probelms, not just wring a little extra revenue out of some very familiar business using a mobile app.

We can do better than that. In fact, we must in order to create the world that will come later, whenever the pandemic inevitably ends.

On to the latest Web 3.0 headlines:

  • One moral emerging from the pandemic story already is that free speech matters. Further details emerged regarding the Chinese Communist Party's initial suppression of information about coronavirus beginning in December, which demostrably cost the whole world precious time in preventing spread of the disease. Both the U.S. and China have since expelled correspondents from one another's news organizations, which also won't help matters in terms of getting people information they need. Meanwhile, journalists in China used the Ethereum blockchain to immutably publish an interview with a doctor regarding the coronavirus, effectively working around state censors. This is at least the second documented use of Ethereum in such a way in China, including a censor-proof memorial posted last month in honor of the doctor who discovered coronavirus.
