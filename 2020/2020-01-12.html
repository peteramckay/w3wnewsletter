Week in Review

Jan. 5-11, 2020

As it happens, a lot of the news this time is about meetups, conferences, and livestreams around Web 3.0.

It's fascinating to me that in this industry ‚Äî the whole point of which is to produce cutting-edge software and distributed networks ‚Äî there's still such a need to connect in person, the old-fashioned way. Of course, on a bad day, these events can also sidetrack into obnoxiousness, much of it boat-based, as my friend Matt McGraw has previously pointed out.

Here's hoping for more productive collaboration, less nonsense, in 2020.

Then he'll pay back the loan over time to token holders, which could possibly include any Nets fan with a crypto account.

The idea is getting surprisingly bipartisan support on Capitol Hill, though it may not be enough to save many papers even if it's enacted, Benton says.
  • Speaking of the feds, I just belatedly discovered that the Federal Trade Commission last year published official advice on how to avoid SIM swapping, an increasingly common hacker attack on mobile devices. Who knew?
