Let's get to some headlines:

  • Ethereum continues to rule decentralized finance, although some analysts are concerned about the longer term. CoinDesk's Danny Nelson reported that stablecoin issuer Tether shifted $1 billion of its USDT token from the Tron blockchain to Ethereum on Thursday, sapping more than one-fifth of Tran's total USDT reserves. (Link: https://tinyurl.com/y4ujoxdm) However, Cointelegraph fretted that "a storm is brewing" for Ethereum as mining has slowed and longtime holders of ETH tokens have historically strong incentives to sell right now.   • ICYMI: My former Dow Jones colleague Mike Casey recently weighed in on the pros and cons of modern monetary theory, especially in light of the current virus-driven economic downturn. While most crypto folks are skeptical of MMT, which advocates open-ended printing of fiat money as a form of economic stimulus, Casey says the "dangerous idea" might have some merit under present circumstances. (Link: https://tinyurl.com/y3dub36e) I've generally been (and remain) skeptical of MMT myself, frankly. (Link: https://tinyurl.com/y6nebnt6) But I also think it's good to give other perspectives a fair hearing, especially when they're coming from someone with Mike's level of experience.
  • CoinDesks Leigh Cuen took a look at Cuba's growing bitcoin community. She paints a picture that neatly encapsulates many of the use cases that crypto proponents hold dear, including easy cross-border payments and empowering people without traditional bank accounts, especially in poorer countries. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y2yb7jog
