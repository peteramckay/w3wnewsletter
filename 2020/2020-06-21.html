June 14-20, 2020

Let's talk dystopia movies for a second.

I never thought of Jurassic Park in that category, but re-watching it on cable recently for the first time in years, I realized it does indeed belong.

Thing is, Jurassic Park doesn't show us a scary urbanized society like Blade Runner or V for Vendetta or 1984 ― stories more commonly cited as dystopias. But what it very much has in common with them is characters living with unintended consequences from supposed progress, technological and otherwise.

Recall the famous warning given to Jurassic Park's founder:

"Your scientists were so preoccupied with whether or not they could, they didn't stop to think if they should."

In other words, sometimes people set out to build something awesome, but then things go left. Whether you show that devolution onscreen, as Jurassic Park does, or whether it's implied offscreen in lieu of focusing entirely on the aftermath, as most dystopia movies do, the requirement that there be an "oh, shit" moment somewhere in the narrative is constant.

In real life, we're currently having one collective "oh, shit" moment after another. Minus the dinosaur cloning, of course. For now.

On to the week's news:

  • Six civil rights groups joined forces to urge Facebook advertisers to boycott the platform to curb the spread of hate and misinformation. Outdoor goods retailers North Face and REI immediately signed up for the campaign, which is using the hashtag #StopHateforProfit. (CNET) Link: https://tinyurl.com/yc2o52ul
  • Christopher Giancarlo, former chairman of the Commodity Futures Trading Commission, spoke at length with podcaster Laura Shin about why he believes the U.S. needs an official "digital dollar." He recently created the Digital Dollar Foundation to push the idea in Washington. Link: https://tinyurl.com/yce5mabm
  • Gideon Lichfield, editor of the MIT Technology Review, said the U.S. may eventually become a "pariah state" due to its bungled COVID-19 response, with other countries imposing special travel restrictions on Americans to protect their own citizens. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y9cq5fsy
  • Polkadot has launched a new token redeemable for bitcoin, called PolkaBTC. The project is the latest of several efforts industrywide to add cross-chain compatability that would allow Ethereum and other networks to tap the overwhelming popularity of bitcoin. Conversely, cross-chain integrations could add programatic power to bitcoin as a software platform ― a current weak point for the popular token. It currently comprises about two-thirds of the $266 billion global crypto market. (CoinDesk) Link: https://tinyurl.com/yaz9xsp6
  • Ripple, Brave, and Huobi have joined the Open Payment Coalition, which operates the instant payment network PayID. The coalition's goal is to make sending money as easy as sending an email or text message for over 100 million consumers. (The Merkle) Link: https://tinyurl.com/ya8bpmrp
  • One of my consulting clients had some cross-chain news of their own: Telos has added an Ethereum Virtual Machine that allows developers to move their smart contracts over from the number-two blockchain network. Woot! Link: https://tinyurl.com/y8bapsgu
  • The code-sharing platform GitHub will drop the term "master" as the default branch name from its hosted repositories in response to protests about ingrained racism. (The Register) Link: https://tinyurl.com/y7ncnnqe
  • TechCrunch's Ingrid Lunden reports that Stockwell, the vending machine startup originally known as Bodega, is shutting down July 1. Thank God. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y9kju3kr
