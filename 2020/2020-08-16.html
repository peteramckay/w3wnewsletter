Web 3.0 Weekly

Aug. 9-15, 2020

No doubt about it, decentralized finance increasingly looks like the blockchain space's new killer app. Several recent developments show it taking on more functions akin to those of traditional banks:

  • Bitcoin is becoming a DeFi juggernaut. CoinDesk's Zack Voell reports that more bitcoins have been wrapped and put on the Ethereum chain lately than created through mining. (Link: https://tinyurl.com/y55rd7xn) Wrapped BTC can be used more readily by lending apps running on the Etehreum chain. Separately, Coinbase announced it will allow customers in 17 U.S. states to borrow in dollar terms against up to 30% of their BTC holdings beginning this fall. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y5pcglfz
  • The rapid rise and fall of the YAM token illustrated DeFi's potential pitfalls, however. The project launched Tuesday as a vehicle for an arbitrage strategy called yield farming, quickly grew to a market cap of nearly $500 million, and then crashed 90 percent days later after a bug in the system was discovered. (The Block) Link: https://tinyurl.com/y3bfdufp
  • China has outlined plans for further tests of its new "digital yuan," expanding its pilot program to include Beijing and Hong Kong. (Bloomberg) Link: https://tinyurl.com/y6pyt8bw
  • MicroStrategy, a Nasdaq-listed software company worth $1.2 billion, disclosed in a new regulatory filing that it has purchased $250 million in bitcoin as an inflation hedge. The move could pave the way for other public companies to purchase cryptoassets for the first time, writes fund manager Christopher Brookins. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y48bs2k7
  • The Block's Frank Chaparro reports that J.P. Morgan is in talks to invest $20 million in ConsenSys as part of a $50 million convertible debt funding round. As part of the deal, ConsenSys would take over maintenance and support of Quorum, the banking giant's Ethereum-based blockchain platform. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y6jg46tx (Paywall)
  • The U.S. Justice Department announced it had seized millions of dollars worth of cryptoassets from accounts belonging to al-Qaeda and other terror groups, the agency's largest such crackdown to date on such activity. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y6kr7anj
  • Bloomberg's Gerry Smith reports that cable technicians in the U.S. are increasingly concerned about catching coronavirus from maskless customers during service visits. A handy reminder to wear a mask and/or not subscribe to cable in the first place. It's 2020, people. Streaming works. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y2jhrldd

That's it for now. Thanks for spending some time with the newsletter today! Best wishes for a healthy and productive week ahead.
