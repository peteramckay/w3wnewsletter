Aug. 2-8, 2020

A busy week for decentralization news, most of it the good sort:

  • In its latest earnings report, Square reported that bitcoin trading via its payments app Cash jumped to $875 million in the second quarter, up sevenfold from the year-ago period. The rapid growth is good for Square but perhaps even better news for bitcoin, which is perpetually in need of user-friendly entry points for non-geeks to participate, writes Avi Salzman of Barron's. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y5yd97tg
  • Tech merger activity is heating up. Microsoft is in talks to acquire U.S. operations of TikTok from ByteDance, facing a 45-day deadline from the Trump administration to complete the deal. (Link: https://tinyurl.com/yy7fr3wr) The White House has accused the popular social app of being used by the Chinese government to spy on Americans -- a charge that TikTok denies. Separately, the private-equity giant Blackstone Group announced it will aquire the genealgy site Ancestry.com for $4.7 billion in a deal that I'm sure will do wonders for user privacy. #sarcasm Link: https://tinyurl.com/y542y5n8
  • The New York Times detailed blow-by-blow how a 17-year-old Florida youth pulled off a massive hack of Twitter's administrative controls recently. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y2o2tjx4
  • News sites have been hit hard by the recession, but pockets of media innovation continue. Fast Company's Jared Newman reported on Logically, a startup launching a new browser extension and app in the U.S. to beat back the tide of misinformation ahead of the November election. (Link: https://tinyurl.com/y2pros6w) Over at Wired, Gilad Edelman covered some publishers' surprisngly successful experiments with non-targeted ads -- a pro-privacy approach that should have led to disaster, according to conventional wisdom in Silicon Valley. If the trend grows, it could be a welcome boon for publishers who have had a hard time competing with hyper-targeted ad platforms like Facebook and Google. (Link: https://tinyurl.com/y6rxavpd) Separately, National Public Radio announced that applications to its summer internship program rose almost tenfold after it made the gig a remote opportunity. The surge is a promising sign for diversity across the industry. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y3alyhvt
  • Sen. Amy Klobuchar, D-Minn., took to the Senate floor to advocate wider access to broadband internet in the U.S., including in underserved rural areas. Frankly, little legislative action on this (or anything else tech-related) is likely ahead of the big U.S. election in November. Still, it's always good to see someone in an important position speak out about this problem. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y6ynupbg
