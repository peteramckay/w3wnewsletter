Web 3.0 Weekly

Aug. 30 to Sept. 5, 2020

Honestly, I'm not certain the internet as a whole got less centralized last week. But people's money sure did:

  • Crypto trading volume on decentralized exchanges jumped to $11.6 billion in August, more than double the previous month's activity, according to new data from Dune Analytics. (See https://tinyurl.com/y3xp3ofa) DEX trades take place directly on-chain and in some ways may be harder for regulators to trace than transactions on company-controlled platforms like Coinbase. Much of the recent DEX volume has been driven specifically by the decentralized finance (DeFi) platform Uniswap, though upstart SushiSwap is now trying to steal its thunder. (CoinDesk) Link: https://tinyurl.com/y2hvtwcl
  • DeFi also remains the driving force in the broader Ethereum ecosystem, where transaction fees hit a new daily record. ETH miners generated all-time high fee revenue of $17 million on Tuesday, according to data from The Block Research. The figure was more than 10 times bicoin miners' revenue on Tuesday and almost quadruple ETH's own previous record. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y3a5vmsd
  • Grayscale Investments, the largest crypto fund management firm, tweeted that it now has more than $6 billion in client assets under management. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y6ttf95j
  • The Telos Foundation announced that the application develpment firm Katalyo will tokenize $35 million worth of waterfront real estate in Croatia on the Telos blockchain. The project is potentially a major boon to the Telos platform, whose native token has a market cap of less than $8 million. (Full disclosure: The Telos Foundation is a freelance client of mine.) Link: https://tinyurl.com/y24kk4vv
  • Ian Lee, managing director of IDEO CoLab Ventures, predicted that "fair launches" will disrupt venture capital in the crypto space, allowing more projects to launch without support from Silicon Valley's traditional kingmakers. Fair launches entail no pre-mining of tokens or other features that create outsize gains for founders and other early participants in a new crypto offering. Link: https://tinyurl.com/yyoppwx5
  • The browser maker Brave's research arm published a post summarizing their recent work, including updates on machine learning, data privacy and security, performance, and cryptography. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y4478web
  • In a new Wall Street Journal video report, my friends Lingling Wei and Bob Davis gave a good overview of how China and America are rapidly "decoupling" in tech. Over the next few years, two online ecosystems may emerge, separated from the bare metal hardware layer right up through services, the WSJ says. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y48kbs9x
  • Amazon posted and then hastily deleted job openings for "intelligence analysts" to help the company identify "labor organizing threats" after the listings created a viral uproar on social media. (See Wired magazine's full coverage at https://tinyurl.com/yxsj67lv) A screen capture of the job postings was shared by Athena, an alliance opposing Amazon's increasing economic and societal influence, at https://tinyurl.com/yxqsg6x4
  • Author and digital activist Cory Doctorow published a handy how-to on destroying surveillance capitalism. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y4ssceus
  • A brainstorm: Perhaps we should hire whoever does supply chain for Oreos to handle protective equipment for U.S. hospital workers as well. Seems there are now 58 varieties of those cookies, and every damn one is consistently in stock at the supermarket. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y6pf8cwe

That's it for now. Thanks for spending some time with the newsletter today! Best wishes for a healthy and productive week ahead.
