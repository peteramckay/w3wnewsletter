Web 3.0 Weekly

Aug. 23-29, 2020

At present, it's common to meet someone in tech whose interest in gadgets and computers was sparked in childhood by Star Wars or similar sci-fi movies from past decades.

Similarly, I believe we will someday soon see a wave of technologists, many of them Black or from other underrepresented groups, whose imaginations were ignited early on by Black Panther in 2018. Consider that the movie grossed over $1 billion -- a unicorn in Silicon Valley startup terms -- and was beloved around the world. Its cultural impact ran that deep.

Hence the tragic loss of Chadwick Boseman on Friday at just 43 years old was a real loss for the tech industry in a way I hope everyone in it appreciates. In his way, Boseman was probably more effective at promoting diversity in tech than all the Big Tech executives combined who perennially talk a good game but mysteriously can't ever get the share of Black people among their employees above a single-digit percentage.

It's also beyond remarkable that Boseman's work sparked joy for millions, all while he privately faced down a deadly disease himself without ever uttering a word about it publicly. That's real heroism, no special effects required.

On a clearly related note, the entreprenship organization Black & Brown Founders recently announced it will host a virtual conference on diversity in tech dubbed Chroma from Sept. 28 to Oct. 2. For more information or to sign up to attend, please visit https://tinyurl.com/y4odxtbv

In other news:

  • Fidelity, one of the largest asset management firms in the U.S., filed paperwork with the Securities and Exchange Commission to launch a new fund dedicated to bitcoin. The minimum to invest in the fund will be $100,000 -- a relatively high figure that could keep many retail investors out. But if even a small fraction of the $8.3 trillion in client money that Fidelity manages ends up participating, the fund could nevertheless help to expand the overall audience for bitcoin significantly. (Forbes) Link: https://tinyurl.com/yxgdz56m
  • Barry Silbert's Digital Currency Group said it will invest $100 million into mining bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies in North America over the coming months through its Foundry unit. North America has lagged in mining activity the last few years, lagging in market share versus China and other regions where operating costs are lower. (Fortune) Link: https://tinyurl.com/y6sgg68x
  • America's Internal Revenue Service moved to include a very broad question about crypto activity on 2020 tax forms. Opponents complained the changes raise concerns about privacy and even free speech. (Decrypt) Link: https://tinyurl.com/y3tlofc8
  • Windows 95, one of the defining products of the early dotcom bubble, turned 25 years old. (The Verge) Link: https://tinyurl.com/y4u8vach
  • MarketWatch's Brett Arends weighed in on one of the big downsides of moving from cities to suburbs -- a change many workers in tech and finance are considering amid the recent coronavirus pandemic. Over time, the expense of car ownership may sap up to a half million dollars from one's savings, Arends writes. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y46p4x54
