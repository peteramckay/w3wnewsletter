Web 3.0 Weekly

March 8-14, 2020

There have been many earnest, thoughtful warnings in the media lately about using social distancing and other precautions to prevent the spread of coronavirus.

Then there's the #StayTheFuckHome movement.

As the hashtag implies, this is a somewhat gonzo effort aimed at piercing people's basic propensity not to listen to prudent advice. Once you drill down and read the full text of the #StayTheFuckHome website, you realize its publishers are pretty earnest as well. But they rightly prioritize getting -- and holding -- the attention of people's lizard brains first.

#StayTheFuckHome is also a fundamentally geeky effort, spearheaded by Florian Reifschneider, CTO at Rocketloop, a German tech consultanting firm. He's even open sourced the website via GitHub, with a particular focus on getting translations of the profane-but-important message into other languages.

Seems #StayTheFuckHome is currently still in need of translators for Swedish and Danish in particular. Please do reach out via the project's GitHub repo or email contribute[at]staythefuckhome.com if you're able to help with either of these translations. (If you're fluent in some other language that's missing from their current mix of published translations, I'm sure the team would be open to that contribution as well.)

On to this fucked-up week's other headlines:

  • CoinDesk announced it is going to turn its widely anticipated Consensus 2020 conference into a free online event. Organizers said they will issue refunds within 60 days to anyone who paid to register for the in-person version, previously scheduled for May in New York.
  • Marketing consultant Matt Crook has published a couple of worthy reads lately, including his tips on working remotely and a rant about shady crypto stuff he's witnessed in the industry.
  • An old video of former Federal Reserve Chairman Ben Bernanke prompted a Twitter rant of my own about the monetary fundamentals of the dollar versus bitcoin. Even with the bloodbath in the crypto markets this week, I'll stand by this one.
  • The website Marginal Revolution has posted a community resources page about teaching economics online, for all the university faculty having to ramp up such activity right now.
  • I greatly enjoyed a new podcast episode from the Harvard Business Review this week about why and how capitalists should take a more active role in saving democracy around the world. Coincidentally, right after I listened to this one, financier Ken Chenault decided to leave Facebook's board due to dissatisfaction about the company's governance and political policies, according to the Wall Street Journal. Hey, maybe there's hope yet...
  • On the other hand, in the event that we never do quite figure out the "preserving Western democracy as we know it" thing and/or coronavirus and/or climate change and/or nuclear proliferation, I guess there is still a possibility humanity will need some backup option to this whole Earth deal. Exploring Mars is a hot topic in Silicon Valley these days, advocated by Elon Musk and others. But Saturn's moon Titan is a much better candidate for long-term human colonization, according to a new essay by science journalist Charles Wohlforth and former NASA scientist Amanda R. Hendrix.
