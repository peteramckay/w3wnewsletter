Web 3.0 Weekly

May 17-23, 2020

Is there a holiday more fraught than Memorial Day? Many Americans welcome it each year as the unofficial start of summer, with all the fun stuff that entails. But the day of course also has its more somber connotation as a time to honor the sacrifice of our fallen veterans.

This year, I notice the holiday is being taken as a time to mourn fallen civilians to some degree as well, since American deaths from COVID-19 happen to be approaching the grim 100,000-person milestone right now.

The New York Times yesterday dedicated its entire front page and then some in print to a simple but striking list of all the victims' names. (See an image of it at https://tinyurl.com/yatjm9zl) The project was accompanied online by a powerful interactive package detailing exactly how, where, and why the U.S. death toll has risen the last few months. (Link: https://tinyurl.com/y8dr4gn5)

At the same time, various states are beginning to experiment with easing restrictions on the operation of brick-and-mortar businesses and social distancing. Which I guess is why I wanted to mention the virus up front this time.

If you're reading this, I want you to enjoy your summer, by all means. But please do remember to continue taking precautions against the virus as well, based upon not just official guidance but a healthy dose of your own common sense. Above all, that's what I want everyone to remember this particular Memorial Day.

On to some tech headlines:

  • CoinDesk's David Pan reports that top Chinese political advisers have proposed a regional digital currency that would be backed by four major Asian denominations -- the Japanese yen, the Korean won, the Hong Kong dollar and mainland China's yuan. Link: https://tinyurl.com/yavjt45u
  • On Wednesday, Goldman Sachs is set to host a conference call for clients about bitcoin, gold, and inflation. As the invitation circulated on Wall Street last week, it raised eyebrows among BTC bulls, who are hoping that upbeat words from Goldman about the cryptocurrency may translate into new buying in bitcoin at a time when its price has paused around $9,000. (Forbes) Link: https://tinyurl.com/yba2bppc
  • Four Democratic senators raised antitrust concerns in a letter to the Justice Department about Uber’s recent offer to acquire Grubhub, the biggest competitor to the company's existing Uber Eats service. (TechCrunch) Link: https://tinyurl.com/y6utcsl2
  • Corporate bankruptcies are piling up amid the economic fallout from COVID-19, including recent filings from J.C. Penney, Hertz, Nordstrom's, and Gold's Gym. Meanwhile, Amazon shares are trading at an all-time high on expectations they'll soon report a big sales increase. This inspired a rant by comedian Bill Maher on his show Friday that I think works as a brief, layman-friendly summary of the situation. Maher said: "When one supplier brings you everything, you're not a customer. You're a dependent." Link: https://tinyurl.com/ycavcolz
  • Rant #2 worth reading this week: Former CIA operative-turned-local-cop Patrick Skinner's tweetstorm ripping federal law enforcement's aversion to obtaining warrants for people's information. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y8zztxnx
  • Finally, a quick note about a worthy (and timely) cause: My friend Sabrina Patrick-Urrutia is raising money for the nonprofit Mission Neighborhood Health Center in San Francisco. They provide care for one of the city's most diverse neighborhoods, with a heavy emphasis lately on COVID testing and prevention, of course. To support them, please visit http://www.mnhc.org/donate/
