Web 3.0 Weekly

May 10-16, 2020

Seems the blockchain tail is wagging the dog this week. The latest headlines, laden with plot twists:

  • Visa has filed a patent to launch a digital currency on the Ethereum blockchain. The timetable when such a token might go live is unclear. However, the patent filing is a clear sign Visa remains interested in the stablecoin space despite its recent decision to leave the Facebook-led Libra Consortium. Looks like they may go it alone instead. (FXStreet) Link: https://tinyurl.com/y7omjysr
  • My old colleague Paul Vigna reports in the Wall Street Journal that J.P. Morgan Chase has begun offering corporate banking services to two crypto exchanges — Coinbase and Gemini. We've come a long way indeed from the days when the bank's CEO Jamie Dimon said bitcoin was a "fraud" that would inevitably "blow up." (Paywall) Link: https://tinyurl.com/ycdwqcq7
  • Bitcoin's "halving" happened as expected on Monday, cutting the rewards miners receive for creating new tokens. The event effectively preserves the overall scarcity of bitcoin — a dynamic that has pushed the token's price back near $10,000 and garnered fresh curiosity among investors big and small. In an interview on CNBC, renowned hedge fund manager Paul Tudor Jones called bitcoin "a great speculation" and disclosed he's allocated over 1 percent of his own portfolio to it. Link: https://tinyurl.com/yddqpzje
  • Telegram abandoned development of a new blockchain platform, the Telegram Open Network, due to disputes with U.S. regulators. (TechCrunch) Link: https://tinyurl.com/yd2f3k65
  • The Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers, the non-profit organization that oversees the internet's domain name system, has rejected a controversial proposal to sell the .org domain to a private equity group for more than $1 billion. (Ars Technica) Link: https://tinyurl.com/y7hj34mo
  • Reddit launched a new feature called Community Points, which uses the Ethereum chain to reward users for curating news content. (Mashable) Link: https://tinyurl.com/yaccxyp4
  • At CoinDesk's Consensus:Distributed conference, former U.S. Treasury Secretary Larry Summers raised eyebrows by saying there's currently "too much privacy" around people's money, not too little, as many of the blockchain industry types in attendance tend to believe. Link: https://tinyurl.com/ycw9wd3r
  • Mathemetician and author Cathy O'Neil warned that non-public algorithms in use at the Federal Emergency Management Agency to make decisions about the COVID-19 crisis are putting citizens at additional risk. Link: https://tinyurl.com/ycxxdyas
  • Tech investor and web pioneer Marc Andreessen issued a broad call to action on innovation to address societal problems highlighted by the COVID-19 crisis. "It's time to build," he wrote. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y93froxf
