Web 3.0 Weekly

July 5-11, 2020

The politician Rahm Emanuel once famously said, "Never let a crisis go to waste."

The tech world is clearly taking that idea to heart lately, for both better and worse, I think:

  • Reuters reported that Coinbase has begun planning an initial public offering on the stock market, possibly by the end of the year. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y7l3syw4
  • Decentralized finance remains red hot. According to data from Dune Analytics, about 240,000 users are now active in the space, with more than $2 billion in assets deposited. The number of "DeFi" protocols has more than doubled this year, although activity is heavily concentrated in just the top five. (Link: https://tinyurl.com/y6wlozhq) Among the notable new entrants is Aave, which launched a service on Ethereum last week that lets users take out loans without collateral. (Link: https://tinyurl.com/y89an53m) Meanwhile, VC Dan Elitzer took an in-depth look at the increasing phenomenon of "yield farming" on the DeFi platforms. Scroll down at https://tinyurl.com/ybxkj28k for his full take.
  • Ethereum transaction fees have been soaring as a percentage of miner revenue, far surpassing Bitcoin's fees despite the recent halving, according to Larry Cermak, research director at The Block. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y7juxqyt
  • American law enforcement agencies have been buying personal information about citizens from data brokers that they would normally need a warrant to obtain. Sen. Ron Wyden is proposing a law to ban the practice. (Motherboard) Link: https://tinyurl.com/y8rhn3gz
  • The Chinese Communist Party's efforts to thwart democracy are growing more aggressive both at home and abroad. Vice's David Gilbert reports that the "Great Firewall" is beginning to encircle Hong Kong, with major U.S. tech platforms likely to be blocked soon. (Link: https://tinyurl.com/ybd6bc3o) In Beijing, authorities arrested law professor Xu Zhangrun, who was previously critical of president Xi Jinping's handling of the COVID pandemic. (Link: https://tinyurl.com/y7uzckoh) Meanwhile, FBI director Christopher Wray said at an event at the Hudson Institute in Washington that his agency is now opening a new China-related counterintelligence investigation approximately every 10 hours. American healthcare organizations, pharmaceutical companies, and academic institutions are among the organizations increasingly targeted by Chinese spying, Wray said. Link: https://tinyurl.com/ycjzwkud
  • Tether has blacklisted more than three dozen Ethereum addresses at the request of law enforcement, rendering millions of dollars of USDT tokens held by those addresses unusable. (The Block) Link: https://tinyurl.com/yavfgsqz
  • Arca Labs says it has launched an SEC-approved fund that tracks U.S. Treasury bonds using the Etehreum ledger. The offering is apparently the first token-based investment approved under the Investment Company Act of 1940 by the Securies and Exchange Commission, which has generally opposed such vehicles in the past. (CoinDesk) Link: https://tinyurl.com/ycpv6b47
  • A who's who of crypto startups received loans totaling over $30 million under the U.S. government's Paycheck Protection Program (PPP), writes CoinDesk's Nikhilesh De. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y988lovr
  • TikTok saw an organized effort to pump the price of Dogecoin. Groan... (The Block) Link: https://tinyurl.com/ybhrnslh
  • Margaret Harding McGill of Axios reports that a broadband program for low-income internet users in the U.S. has been grossly undertilized. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y8b2xfws
  • Finally, a heads-up about a great cause: SMASH, an education program by the Kapor Center to help youth from underrepresented groups who are interested in tech, has launched a new donor drive. Through July 31, founders Mitch Kapor and Freada Kapor Klein will be personally matching your donations. Please help 'em out! Link: https://tinyurl.com/ybaursw6

That's it for now. Thanks for spending some time with the newsletter today! Best wishes for a healthy and productive week ahead.
