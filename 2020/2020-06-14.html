Web 3.0 Weekly

June 7-13, 2020

Wow. A pretty busy week this time in terms of news flow. Here are the highlights:

  • The The New York Times' Mike Isaac reports that Big Tech companies have been on a startup acquisition spree lately amid the onset of recession in the U.S. and elsewhere. The dealmaking suggests that Facebook, Apple, and other goliath-sized peers smell opportunity in the woes of smaller players and non-tech industries to consolidate their market power even further. Link: https://tinyurl.com/yckx29c9
  • About half of institutional investors consider digital assets to be worthy of holding in portfolios, according to a recent survey commissioned by Fidelity Investments. (Bloomberg) Link: https://tinyurl.com/y47sgutv
  • The hashtag #DeleteCoinbase has been trending recently as portions of the blockchain community revolt against the popular exchange's deal to provide analytics to the U.S. government. Coinbase has said its government work will only involve use of public on-chain data, not private information from individual users of Coinbase's proprietary platform. But that's been little comfort to the most ardent crypto enthusiasts, many of whom lean toward libertarianism, anarchism, or other anti-authoritarian belief systems. Link: https://tinyurl.com/ycazqkmk
  • CoinDesk reports that ConsenSys is launching a new regulatory compliance product called Codefi, which will analyze transactions in the growing decentralized finance (DeFi) space. Link: https://tinyurl.com/ya95yj2z
  • Microsoft announced that its decentralized identity tool, called ION, is moving to the Bitcoin mainnet for a public beta test. (The Block) Link: https://tinyurl.com/y85hqbef
  • The Human Rights Foundation announced it has launched a fund to "support software developers who are making the Bitcoin network more private, decentralized, and resilient so that it can better serve as a financial tool for human rights activists, civil society organizations, and journalists around the world." The foundation is trying to raise $50,000 for the fund through a crowdfunding campaign. To donate, visit https://tinyurl.com/yayn7b84
  • Software engineer @LareneLg has been tweeting a lot lately about web accessibility, including a heartbreaking story about her father's struggles to use sites that haven't taken steps to accommodate the visually impaired. Definitely worth a look, especially for the designers out there. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y88cwnol
  • Of course, some people still use Twitter for certain less-serious purposes as well. For instance, British journalist @iancanwrite's hilarious (and nervy) pranking of the CIA's official account last week. Be sure to click through the several screenshots he posted to get the full story. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y7n2lefe
  • Variety reports that Cameron and Tyler Winklevoss are teaming with Stampede Ventures on a feature film adaptation of the 2019 book “Bitcoin Billionaires.” Its author, Ben Mezrich, also wrote "The Social Network," which was turned into a movie that portrayed the Winklevoss brothers' conflict with Facebook founder Mark Zuckerberg. Link: https://tinyurl.com/ybbrwg9m
