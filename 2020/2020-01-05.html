Week in Review

Dec. 29, 2019 to Jan. 4, 2020

Happy New Year! News on Web 3.0 has already been breaking at a rapid clip in 2020, so let's get to it:

  • My friend Steve Russolillo, who covers blockchain for the Wall Street Journal out of Hong Kong, has done a five-minute video explainer about China's plan to launch a national cryptocurrency. Great background here if you're just getting up to speed on this story, with some new detail as well for the OGs out there. ðŸ˜‰
  • Bitcoinist reports that block times on Ethereum have dropped about 25 percent over the past month, from an average of 17 seconds to 12.69 seconds to confirm each transaction. Woot!
  • Over on CoinDesk, Christine Kim says data show that gambling and gaming have been outpacing decentralized finance recently as popular use cases for blockchain apps.
  • Jan. 3 marked the 11-year anniversary of the genesis block on the Bitcoin network. According to Bitcoinist, the network carried $673 billion in transaction volume in 2019, including some single transactions of more than $1 billion. Bitcoin's growth has also outpaced that of many fiat-based payment systems, including Venmo.
  • The Telos Foundation, a marketing client of mine, has published its first-ever annual report, summarizing major developments in 2019. There's also a Twitter chat scheduled this Tuesday, Jan. 7 at 12 UTC with Doug Horn, architect of the Telos blockchain. Fuller details available here.
  • Former MakerDAO contributor Nikolai Mushegian announced on New Year's Eve that he's donated $1.3 million in MKR tokens to his alma mater, Carnegie Mellon University, for research into distributed applications and protocols. He's also formally commited to additional gifts of MKR over the next three years that could push the total donation over $4 million.
  • The annual CES conference, a staple of the tech industry since the 1960s, starts Tuesday in Las Vegas. A handy listing of this year's crypto-specific conference sessions is available here.
  • MIT Technology Review reports that ransomware may have cost the U.S. government agencies and businesses more than $7.5 billion in 2019. Ouch.
  • Major national newspapers are issuing increasingly dire warnings about online privacy. In a video featuring star tech columnists Farhad Manjoo and Kara Swisher, the the New York Times opinion page argues that everyone "should be freaking out" about privacy right now. In particular, they try to de-bunk the common "I have nothing to hide" mentality that leads users to give away their data wantonly. Meanwhile, the Washington Post's Geoffrey A. Fowler weighed in with some thoughts on how we can "survive the surveillance apocolypse."
  • Loved this little factoid recently tweeted by Bloomberg's Joe Weisenthal: Over the last 12 months, shares of Goldman Sachs have been tightly correlated to the S&P sector index tracking the... wait for it... casino industry.
  • Finally, count me among the geeks disappointed in Rise of Skywalker. But on the bright side, the movie's release did prompt this hilarious guide by Slate's Matthew Dessem covering how to kill your enemies so they stay dead in the Star Wars universe. Glad someone was willing to provide a little clarity on that front.

That's it for now. Thanks for spending some time with Indizr today!
