Web 3.0 Weekly

April 26 to May 2, 2020

Hello again. You may notice, I'm trying something different with links to news stories this week. Shortened URLs are displayed inline rather than as highlighted hypertext within each summary item below.

The idea is to make third-party content easier to find for readers who are interested in learning more about each story. I've had a few folks ask about this recently, so I figured a little experiment might be in order.

We'll see how it goes. I always say, the newsletter is a work in progress.

Now the fun part. Let's get to the headlines:

  • CNBC reports that China has begun a pilot of its new official cryptocurrency in the Xiong'an area. Nineteen companies are reportedly participating in the test run, including McDonald's, Starbucks, and Subway. Link: https://tinyurl.com/yag2avtr
  • The newly distributed (and free) version of the popular Consensus conference is coming up May 11-15. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y7swdg8o
  • CoinMarketCap celebrated its seventh anniversary. The widely followed market data site was recently acquired by Binance and has expanded into hosting branded events. Link: https://tinyurl.com/yd8og8gb
  • Speaking of markets, Laura Shin's podcast Unconfirmed recently took a comprehensive look at the state of bitcoin trading. Guest Mike McGlone, senior commodity strategist at Bloomberg Intelligence, said BTC is clearly maturing as an asset, playing more of a "safe haven" role of late after an initial rout in March driven by coronavirus fears. Link: https://tinyurl.com/yaus5uoy
  • Due to ongoing litigation with U.S. regulators, the messaging app Telegram delayed the launch of its new token until April 2021 and offered to return $1.2 billion to investors. (CoinDesk) Link: https://tinyurl.com/ybngfkbw
  • The venture giant Andreessen Horowitz closed its second crypto fund, with $515 million in capital committed. The new fund comes at a time when the firm will have less competition to invest in startups, as some of its peers have retreated from crypto, TechCrunch notes. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y74qqygg
  • The World Economic Forum published a comprhensive toolkit (edited in part by yours truly) to help supply-chain organizations implement blockchain technology. Link: https://tinyurl.com/ybtxhwx7
  • Writing for Harvard Business Review, Rita Gunther McGrath and Ryan McManus offered a handy explainer of "discovery-driven digital transformation" -- an innovation framework that may be particularly useful for companies who have to adapt rapidly due to the coronavirus crisis. Link: https://tinyurl.com/ydhpvgwq
  • Money manager Barry Ritholtz offered some thoughts about how the U.S. government could respond more comprehensively to ease the economic pain of social distancing. Building upon the historic example of the New Deal, the federal government should perhaps invest aggressively in mitigating climate change, sustainable energy, infrastructure, smart roads, digital healthcare, or even astroid mining, Ritholtz says. (Bloomberg Businessweek) Link: https://tinyurl.com/yb7gqzf3
  • Finally, congrats to Stanford engineering professor Andrea Goldsmith, the first woman to win the Marconi Prize, a prestigous award for communications innovation. (Forbes) Link: https://tinyurl.com/ya9s5l4j
