Week in Review: Nov. 3-9, 2019


As I was working on new content for the Indizr blog the other day, a quote by James Baldwin that a long-ago writing coach shared with me came to mind:

“When you're writing, you're trying to find out something which you don't know.”

I love that. It also makes me wistful that Baldwin, who left us in 1987, didn't live long enough to have a blog or newsletter. With that approach, I think he would've been really, really good at it.

OK, on to the latest Web 3.0 news, including Indizr's own content and headlines from elsewhere around the web:

  • Big changes are coming to the MakerDAO ecosystem on Nov. 18. The decentralized finance project will begin accommodating multi-collateral positions that enable users to mint the DAI stablecoin. Ether is currently the only form of collateral allowed for this purpose in the MakerDAO system, but the new setup will allow up to six other tokens to be accepted as well. Seems like a really handy feature to expand the use of MakerDAO, though some community members have chafed at the introduction of new terminology in the upcoming multi-collateral system.  
  • Among my friends in journalism, paywalls are quite a hot topic right now. Here's why I'm skeptical they'll be a cure-all, especially for local publishers who have been in crisis lately. Better to focus on models that are more experimental, including events, local e-commerce, memberships, or blockchain. Speaking of which...
  • Belated thanks to Daniel Colin James of GiveBAT for featuring Indizr on their homepage, including a very thoughtful review of our content so far.  GiveBAT is a handy curated listing of sites using Basic Attention Token and the Brave web browser to monetize content outside the dreaded Google/Facebook advertising duopoly. Please do give check their site out, especially if you're a news junkie like me.
  • It strikes me that those of us who are passionate about decentralization really need to do a better job framing it for non-geeks specifically as a solution to the evils of Big Tech that have ticked everyone off so mightily the last few years. Speaking of which...
  • In his latest New Rules segment on HBO, comedian Bill Maher encouraged users to get off Facebook. He's been critical of the company before, but never this direct about encouraging viewers to take specific action. My only quibble with Maher's remarks is that he focused so much on the flagship Facebook app that he forgot to mention Facebook-owned properties like Instagram, WhatsApp, and Messenger. Remember to dump them too, lest you remain in Zuck's clutches by a different means.
  • A quick mobile tip: Airplane mode isn't just for airplanes. You can also use it as an all-purpose digital "do not disturb" sign anytime to help boost your productivity, reduce stress, and increase focus.
  • Rachel Tobac, CEO of the infosec firm SocialProof Security, warned against using consumer DNA tests or giving them as gifts this holiday season. "You can’t control how the results will be used in the future, and the results can affect the rest of your family," she tweeted.
  • ZDNet reports that  DNS-over-HTTPS will begin rolling out soon despite opposition by internet service providers. This is basically a big win for user privacy, as it means that the information about web addresses you're visiting can be encrypted at the network layer. You will have to enable the feature and do some configuration as a user, however; it won't be automatic.
  • "Awww, I feel so bad for the ISPs," said no one ever.
  • The Global Blockchain Business Council will host a demo day in Washington on Nov. 13 aimed at legislators, though the event is open to the public. It will feature demonstrations across a range of sectors, including e-governance, financial services, supply chain, energy and more. You can RSVP here.
  • Good to see at least some Brits are maintaining a characteristically eccentric sense of humor through the recent Brexit chaos.

That's it for now. Thanks for spending some time with Indizr today! Remember, if you're just getting started with cryptocurrency and/or blockchain applications, we've got some cool free offers from affiliates that may be of help.

Cheers,

- Peter A. McKay
