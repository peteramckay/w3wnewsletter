Kick Big Tech out of your digital life in 2019

Happy New Year! To start 2019 off with a bang, we already have a bunch of Web 3.0 news to cover from around the web. Here goes:

  • In a new post on the Indizr blog, I've listed 20 tips to nudge Big Tech out of your life in 2019. These are all things that have been on my mind awhile, so I just decided to collect them in one place while everyone's in a mode to make fresh starts. For instance, why not try a new search engine that doesn't track you like a certain one based in Mountain View does?
  • I've been belatedly catching up via YouTube on some of the presentations from the recent Web3 Summit in Berlin. One big highlight so far, for several reasons, was this talk by Protocol Labs CEO Juan Benet covering the basics of what Web 3.0 is. His remarks focused on blockchain, but I was glad to hear him also shout out Sir Tim Berners-Lee's work on semantic HTML as well as a potential building block of the new web. That broad-based definition is something I've tried to follow on the Indizr blog, so I'm glad to hear independent validation of it
  • A talk by Ben Livshits, chief scientist at Brave Software, about the new Brave web browser was also fascinating. The Brave team's vision of using their browser, which includes a built-in crypto wallet, as an "on-ramp" for everyday folks to participate in Web 3.0 is really compelling since mass adoption has been a big problem so far.
  • Ajit Tripathi, a partner at the Ethereum accelerator ConsenSys, is predicting a big year for enterprise blockchain tokens that support real use cases, in contrast to the more purely speculative initial coin offerings of the last few years.
  • Bloomberg's Shira Ovide tweeted to point out that Apple has lost "a Facebook of market value" recently. To be more specific, Apple's market cap is down more than $400 billion since its quarterly earnings report Nov. 1 amid concerns about a potential long-term decline in demand for new iPhones. Seems people are holding onto their devices longer and seeing less reason to upgrade each product cycle as Apple's improvements to the device have become more incremental. Ouch.
  • On a much happier note, the Guardian recently caught up with some of the finalists for the Royal Academy of Engineering's Africa prize, which provides early-stage funding and mentorship for inventors on the continent. A lot of really cool, inspiring stuff here, including a Kenyan man who created a glove with sensors that translate sign language into text.

That's it for now. Thanks for spending some time with Indizr today!

- Peter A. McKay
