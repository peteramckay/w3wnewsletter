Today I'm launching Indizr's first crowdfunding campaign, to help it grow in the New Year as a voice for Web 3.0. Please donate today via GoFundMe to help us spread the word about a more decentralized vision of the internet.

As I've said before, my loose definition of "Web 3.0", which is still a pretty new term in the tech industry, is that it encompasses anything that helps to decentralize digital services and information.

In other words, Web 3.0 tech works 180 degrees the opposite of what most of us experience on the internet these days -- centralization run amok, with just a few big platforms like Google and Facebook dominating our digital lives.

It doesn't have to be this way. The hottest technology right now that supports decentralization -- and the one whose community has most aggressively embraced the term Web 3.0 -- is the blockchain . But I would say other things might also still plausibly be included under the Web 3.0 umbrella at this verrrrrry early stage. Stuff like semantic HTML, which has notably been promoted by worldwide web inventor Sir Tim Berners-Lee via his open-source project Solid  at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and his startup Inrupt. I'd say the open Linux  operating system is also very Web 3.0 at its heart. And there's other stuff out there as well.

I've been writing about these topics and more for a little over a year here on Indizr, which I started as a personal passion project. But I'd like to devote more time to building it into something bigger in 2019, in light of the growing public interest these times around the harm caused by Big Tech platforms.

Won't you please help Indizr grow by making a donation? Your support will help enable me to spend more time on both the Indizr website and this expanded newsletter versus other freelancing or consulting gigs to pay bills this year. That's really the biggest obstacle I have to growing the site right now -- time demands.
