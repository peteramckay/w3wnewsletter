### Week in Review: Nov. 16-22, 2025

- **Bitcoin is on track for its worst month since 2022, with a 33% decline from its all-time high, as a combination of selling pressures and negative catalysts continue to weigh on the crypto market.** The market is experiencing a wave of margin calls, with analysts warning that a break below $82,000 could confirm a bearish trend, and uncertainty around interest rate cuts is adding to the uncertainty. https://www.businessinsider.com/bitcoin-price-why-is-crypto-down-btc-margin-calls-fed-2025-11

- The crypto market has taken a significant hit, with over $1 trillion in value erased since early October, as Bitcoin and other risk assets continue to plummet due to a combination of technical and macro pressures. The market remains fragile, with some analysts predicting a possible local bottom, but others warn of further declines if buyers don't step in decisively. https://www.businessinsider.com/crypto-market-meltdown-erased-1-trillion-bitcoin-btc-digital-assets-2025-11

- The cryptocurrency market has taken a significant hit, with Bitcoin losing over 10% of its value in 24 hours and plummeting to its lowest levels since April. This sell-off has left the entire market reeling, sparking renewed debate about the long-term viability of cryptocurrency as an asset. https://www.axios.com/2025/11/21/bitcoin-value-price-crypto-coin-sell-off

- Tom Lee, a well-known expert at Fundstrat, believes that cryptocurrency prices can serve as a leading indicator for U.S. stock market trends. This perspective highlights the potential connections between the two often-separate markets. https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/20/fundstrats-tom-lee-says-crypto-is-a-leading-indicator-for-us-stock-prices.html

- According to a recent report from JPMorgan, the current crypto market correction is largely driven by retail investors selling spot bitcoin and ether ETFs, with around $4 billion pulled from these investments in November. This selling behavior stands in contrast to strong equity buying, suggesting that retail investors still treat crypto and equities as separate risk assets. https://www.theblock.co/post/379840/jpmorgan-crypto-market-correction-retail-selling-bitcoin-ether-etfs
