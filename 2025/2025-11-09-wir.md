# Bitcoin's bloodbath continues.
## Crypto's biggest bellwether usually rallies in the fall. But not this year, as worries about the U.S. economy are exacting a steep toll.

![A physical bitcoin token surrounded by gold nuggets](https://w3w.news/img/btc-gold-kanchanara-unsplash-3000.jpg)
*Photo by Kanchanara via [Unsplash](https://unsplash.com/@kanchanara)*

**Turns out the market plunge that bitcoiners dubbed "Red October"** has spilled into November as well, fueled by rising worries about the U.S. economy.

Some highlights:

- **On Tuesday,** bitcoin briefly traded below $100,000 per token for the first time since May. ([Investopedia](https://www.investopedia.com/bitcoin-s-price-dropped-below-usd100-000-today-for-the-first-time-in-months-11843005))

- **Heading into Monday's trading session on Wall Street,** U.S.-listed bitcoin funds have seen net outflows of investor money in seven out of eight trading days. That includes $558 million pulled on Friday alone. ([The Block](https://www.theblock.co/post/378098/u-s-spot-bitcoin-etfs-see-largest-daily-outflow-since-august-as-btc-price-hovers-around-100000))

- **Bitcoin fell nearly 5%** in October, its first decline for that month since 2018. So far this month, the token has already shed another 5%. ([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/finance/bitcoin-breaks-october-streak-with-first-monthly-loss-since-2018-2025-10-31/))

- **Bitcoin's plunge has prompted some firms**, including crypto stalwarts Galaxy Digital and ARK Invest, to trim their long-term price forecasts for the token. ([TheStreet](https://www.thestreet.com/crypto/trading/live-analysts-trim-bitcoin-price-targets))

- **But JPMorgan analysts are a notable exception, viewing the selloff as a buying opportunity.** In a Wednesday report, the bank's analysts forecast that bitcoin could [climb to $170,000](https://www.theblock.co/post/377891/jpmorgan-bitcoin-price-170000-next-6-12-months) over the next 6-12 months, based on the token's volatility-adjusted comparison to gold.

As an old commodities reporter, I have to say that comparison to the yellow metal is of particular interest to me right now. More on that in next week's letter.

### Week in Review: Nov. 2-8, 2025

- **Stablecoins, stablecoins everywhere:** A senior British central banker [urged closer collaboration](https://www.reuters.com/sustainability/boards-policy-regulation/bank-englands-breeden-bangs-drum-uk-us-stablecoin-collaboration-2025-11-05/) between the U.K. and America on stablecoin regulation... Italy's biggest banks said they support the European Central Bank's plan for [a digital euro]( https://www.reuters.com/business/finance/italys-banks-back-digital-euro-want-costs-spread-over-time-2025-11-08/). But they want to spread costs out over time... Polygon and fintech startup Anq are [developing a digital rupee](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/technology/tech-news/polygon-anq-developing-sovereign-backed-indian-stablecoin-model-tied-to-government-securities-say-sources/articleshow/125105642.cms?utm_source=chatgpt.com) token pegged to Indian government securities... In emerging markets, where changing local currencies to U.S. dollars is [a daily routine](https://www.forbes.com/sites/digital-assets/2025/11/07/stablecoin-financial-inclusion-friend-or-foe/ ), current trends suggest local banks could lose as much as $1 trillion in deposits to stablecoin usage by the end of 2028, according to analysts at [Standard Chartered Bank](https://www.sc.com/en/uploads/sites/66/content/docs/SC-CIB-Stablecoins-and-EM.pdf).

- **Renowned "Big Short" financier Michael Burry disclosed** a $1.1 billion bet against AI darlings Nvidia and Palantir Technologies, citing concerns over market bubbles and "circular financing" in the tech industry. The practice entails users artificially inflating activity to qualify for rewards. ### Looking Ahead

- **Ahead of this week's COP30 climate summit,** Bill Gates [urged world leaders](https://www.gatesnotes.com/work/accelerate-energy-innovation/reader/three-tough-truths-about-climate) to shift focus from hitting near-term targets for emission reductions. Instead, Gates says it would be better to focus on limiting climate-related suffering, especially among people in the world's poorest countries. <!-- Me -->

- **Consulting giant McKinsey & Co. published** a major new global survey on [enterprise AI adoption](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai). Among the findings: 88% of organizations now report using AI in at least one business function. But most are still struggling to scale AI programs beyond early experimentation. <!-- Gemini -->

- **The Guardian's TechScape newsletter highlighted** some recently reported [market stats](https://www.theguardian.com/global/2025/nov/04/the-mind-boggling-valuations-of-ai-companies) that demonstrate the truly dizzying financial scale of the AI boom. Behold Nvidia's $5 trillion valuation, Microsoft and Apple at $4 trillion each, Alphabet's first-ever quarter with $100 billion in revenue, and $600 billion in newly announced deals to expand computing capacity.
