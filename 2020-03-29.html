Web 3.0 Weekly

March 22-28, 2020

There's an old saying among journalists that three of anything makes a trend. In other words, three is the magic number of tangible examples of a lowercase-t thing that justifies doing a story declaring it to be an uppercase-t Thing, if you see what I mean.

It struck me this week that truly world-shaking catastropes have become a real Thing. A trend.

In less than 20 years, we've now had three of them by my (admittedly subjective) count: the 9/11 attacks, the 2008 financial crisis, and now coronavirus.

The commonality is that these events transcend mere surface-level badness; they are (or were) all destabilizing, life-changing episodes for broad swaths of humanity. They upend markets and economies and political systems. Any one of them, let alone a combination, would probably work as a plotline in an Avengers movie if it hadn't happened in real life.

Three of those in less than two decades strikes me as a really rapid pace. Offhand, it strikes me we're all going to have to make some pretty radical changes personally and professionally if this level of calamity is going to become a regular feature of the world around us from now on. (And, to think, my Big Three list above doesn't even include anything related to climate change.)

If it sustains, this sort of instability will also have major implications for the internet, which of course is the focus around here. It will deeply affect how people use the network, the things we make for it, the pecking order of companies that are winners and losers, and so on.

This week's headlines illustrate that point pretty neatly, I think. Let's get to them:
