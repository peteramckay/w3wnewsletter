March 6-12, 2022

The war in Ukraine is the first major one that will be fought entirely in the web3 era. Some interesting articles are already being published on the implications of this, so I want to focus on them in this edition of the newsletter.

We'll move on to a few other recent headlines of note regarding decentralization further on, I promise. But first things first...

Blogchain Highlights

  • Narratives Project editor Anna Tyger tried to unpack some of the partisan arguments for or against the U.S. banning Russian oil imports to punish the country for its aggression against Ukraine. Meanwhile, over on the official Narratives Project feed, the staff also examined the overall "strategic melodrama" around Ukraine globally, including statements from different countries in favor of Ukraine, in favor of Russia, or in favor of neutrality.
  • Investor Tom Handy wrote a brief but thorough how-to about donating cryptocurrency to the Ukrainian cause. (Tom has also been active in general posting about different crypto investment issues, all in a way that's accessible to newbies. Great to see for Blogchain as a platform. )
  • Father Josh Miller posted about the role that eyewitness social posts from Ukraine and other forms "open source intelligence" are having in shaping the world's view of the conflict, and ultimately the war itself.
  • @Decentralist highlighted a new report by Credit Suisse analyst Zoltan Pozsar predicting that the war will produce profound changes to the global monetary system. Pozsar describes the emerging order "Bretton Woods III" and says of it: "After this war is over, 'money' will never be the same again… and Bitcoin (if it still exists then) will probably benefit from all this."
  • Our team tweeted some thoughts on how we've started building options for creators to monetize using either crypto or conventional money.

Around the Web

  • The Ukrainian government had received nearly $100 million in crypto donations through Wednesday. It's using the money to buy supplies, including night vision googles and bulletproof vests, CoinDesk reports.
  • The crypto exchange Kraken announced it will distribute $10 million in aid to Ukrainian customers by depositing free bitcoin in their accounts.
  • The European Union said crypto transactions are covered by its new sanctions banning "securities" trading with Russian entities.
  • The research firm Chainalysis made its crypto anctions monitoring tools free to the public.
  • New York Times columnist Kevin Roose frets that bitcoin hasn't held up very well as a hedge against inflation and crisis in the two-plus weeks since Russia invaded Ukraine. On the other hand, a glimpse at the token's longer-term performance is much rosier. For instance, it's risen more than 1,500% since July 2017, when a protester urged viewers to buy bitcoin in a photobomb of congressional testimony by then-Federal Reserve chairwoman Janet Yellen. Just sayin...
  • Ethereum gas fees recently hit a 6-month low, driven in part by a cooling of the market for non-fungible tokens, according to Arcane Research.
  • As the 50th anniversary of the release of The Godfather approaches this month, I think there's only one proper way to celebrate: Cannoli! (And re-watch parties, of course.)
