A big landmark for the newsletter, and for decentralization in general.
View this email in your browser

Feb. 27 - March 5, 2022

Today's edition marks a big milestone for this newsletter, which has been published in a few different forms over the last four-plus years. It covers web3, of course, as the name indicates. But for the first time, the newsletter is actually on web3 as well this week, created from scratch and hosted on a decentralized platform.

I've been thinking about how to do this for some time, for obvious reasons. I looked at a few different systems to pull it off. Then one found me.

Capsule Social began building a web3 publishing platform in early 2021. Late last year, we connected over our shared interest in decentralization, and I soon after joined Capsule's team as Head of Content & Writer Development.

On Tuesday, we launched our new platform, Blogchain. I composed this edition of the newsletter on it, and have now posted it to my personal Blogchain feed, including the tag #web3weekly to set it apart from my other posts.

This is going to be my main workflow going forward. For existing email subscribers to #web3weekly, I'm going to continue using MailChimp for distribution for now, cross-posting from Blogchain to that platform. But eventually we plan to add email functionality to Blogchain, so that function will move over as well.

(No need for you to make any changes, by the way. These messages will just continue to keep coming to your inbox as usual, unless you unsubscribe.)

Ultimately, as I've often said, the reason technical decentralization matters is that it affects creative and economic independence. As long as writers, journalists, activists, and other creators have to rely on centralized Big Tech platforms for hosting and distributing their work, they will always be at Big Tech's mercy in every regard, which is intolerable.

There is just no way around this. We need new "software that creates freedom instead of consuming it," to borrow one of my favorite phrases from Dave Winer, who pioneered blogging in the web1 era.

On Blogchain, content like this post is hosted on a distributed network of nodes, which already makes the information more censorship-resistant. We're also working to add on-chain payments, tokenized community governance, and other features not currently available on traditional centralized platforms.

I'm also happy to report that although Blogchain's user base is still small for now, the early quality of posts on Blogchain has been excellent, covering a staggering variety of topics. With that in mind, I'm going to run through the week's headlines a little differently this time, breaking out the links from Blogchain and other places around the web.

Blogchain Highlights

  • Shaun Cammack, director of the Narrative Project, weighed in on potential fixes to the depolarization movement in the U.S. Based on his work with NP, a journalism nonprofit that covers toxic plitical polarization extensively, Cammack says we often misunderstand the nature of the problem -- and thus the possible solutions.
  • On Capsule's official company account, my colleagues provided full details about how Blogchain is different from other publishing platforms.
  • Law student @Scott has been posting about legal issues around smart contracts and non-fungible tokens. Easily one of my favorite follows on the new platform already.
  • Developer Shawn Gordon laid out the advantages of hiring older tech workers and why we should abolish Daylight Saving Time. Yes and yes!
  • @Joret has already done a few thoughtful posts in Blogchain's philosophy category, including a prediction that humans are unlikely to invent an artificial general intelligence.
  • @Sdaswani laid out his concerns about why the limitations of mobile devices and networks may hamper the potential of web3.
  • Photographer @GuruTheBond shared some breathtaking shots of Kenya's natural wonders.

News From Elsewhere

  • Crypto world is wrangling with how not to become a glaring loophole in the enforcement of new Western sanctions against Russia. MetaMask and Infura said they will not be accessible in "certain areas," though further details were sparse. The U.S. Treasury Department issued new rules to clarify which crypto transactions Americans aren't allowed to make with Russian entities. Meanwhile, trading of ruble-based currency pairs on the crypto exchange Binance surged, suggesting some holders of the Russian currency are indeed using crypto as an escape hatch.
  • Polkadot founder Gavin Wood donated $5.8 million in DOT tokens to Ukraine's war effort, according to The Block.
  • The U.S. Justice Department indicted BitConnect founder Satish Kumbhani, alleging a $2.4 billion Ponzi scheme.
  • A rally in Terra's LUNA token pushed it past ETH to become the second-most staked cryptoasset by dollar value, with $27.1 billion now deposited by users. SOL is the number-one most staked, at $33.8 billion in value, according to data from Staking Rewards.
  • The city of Lugano, Switzerland, will recognize Tether, bitcoin, and its own LVGA Points token as "de facto" legal tender, CoinDesk reports.
  • Randi Zuckerberg (a.k.a. Mark's sister) is very enthusiastic about web3, it seems.
