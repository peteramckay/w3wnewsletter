Nov. 7-13, 2021


A quick social sync to begin this time:

As part of getting started with my new gig at Capsule Social, I’ve now created a public calendar for anyone who wants to chat one-on-one about the platform we're building to push decentralized discourse forward.

Sharing this info on my personal channels for the benefit of any fellow writers out there frustrated by the Big Tech platforms’ grip on our content economy. (Which is all of us, no?)

If you or your organization are interested in using Capsule’s new software, let’s definitely talk! Just book an open slot on my Calendly. I’ll be glad to follow up with a video link, and we can go from there. 

By the way, I used the private alpha version of Capsule's software to draft this very update you're reading. I think you're really going to like the new platform when public beta launches in January.

On to the latest decentralization headlines from elsewhere:

  • Global crypto market capitalization topped $3 trillion for the first time on Monday. The market trimmed its gains later in the week, leaving the worldwide valuation of all tokens combined slightly below the big $3 trillion in recent trading. But valuation has still quadrupled for the year, boosted by increased participation from small and large investors alike. During the week's crypto rally, bitcoin set a new all-time high near $69,000.
  • Governance tokens for the Ethereum Name Service, which helps manage web addresses for sites hosted on the Ethereum network, began trading under the ticker ENS. Market cap has already climbed above $5.5 billion.
  • Decentralized finance trading activity on large exchanges has jumped 550% so far this year. Decrypt reported on the increase, citing new data from Chainalysis.
  • Mastercard partnered with Amber, Bitkub, and CoinJar to offer crypto-linked payment cards in Asia.
  • New crypto wallet options everywhere. The messaging app Discord said it may add integration with the popular MetaMask wallet. Meanwhile, Coinbase launched a browser extension that will allow users to access their wallets with the exchange. And Brave said it will add support for Solana tokens and applications to its privacy-focused web browser.
  • Binance co-founder and CEO Changpeng Zhao said the exchange is weighing whether to move its headquarters to France.
  • The Associated Press published a handy explainer of why inflation fears are growing in the U.S. This one isn't a tech story per se, but I include it here because inflation is of such keen interest to so many crypto fans, especially bitcoiners.
  • Harvard business professor emeritus Shoshana Zuboff wrote an essay for the New York Times about how surveillance capitalism began and then eventually ran amok. A nice overview for anyone who is either new to the topic or wants a quick refresher.
  • Apple CEO Tim Cook said he owns crypto personally. But the hardware giant has no plans to invest in tokens as a company.
  • Rolling Stone magazine is auctioning off two covers as non-fungible tokens.
  • I finally got around to seeing No Time to Die yesterday. Wow. I'm going to need a moment here, folks...
