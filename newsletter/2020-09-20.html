The week's other headlines:

  • The popular yield farming platform Uniswap issued a new governance token on Wednesday, using the symbol UNI. Its market capitalization has already jumped to almost $500 million dollars. Link: https://uniswap.org/blog/uni/
  • Blockchain activity in Africa appears to be gaining momentum. Reuters correspondents Alexis Akwagyiram and Tom Wilson report that weak banking systems and local currencies are prompting users in Nigeria and elsewhere to adopt alternatives for payments and other use cases more readily than in many western countries. (Link: https://tinyurl.com/y5vvhnja) In Senegal, singer Akon announced he's broken ground on construction of a new $6 billion project dubbed Akon City about 60 miles outside the capital Dakar. The singer's plans for the new city include a local economy that heavily integrates Akoin, his own branded cryptocurrency built on the Stellar blockchain. Link: https://tinyurl.com/yxoc2t32
  • ConsenSys recently unveiled a new MetaMask mobile app that not only acts as an Ethereum wallet but also a browser for distributed applications. Link: https://tinyurl.com/yykta7y4
  • Coinbase co-founder and CEO Brian Armstrong published a tweetstorm about his company's struggles with Apple's restrictions on mobile developers who want to publish their software in the behemoth's App Store. Apple is facing a mounting revolt among developers since Epic Games, the creator of Fortnite, recently accused the device maker of anticompetitive practices. For its part, Apple maintains that its strict vetting of releases in the App Store is a form of quality control conducted on end users' behalf. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y336bjn7
  • CoinDesk's Omkar Godbole reports that new bitcoin wallets are being created at the fastest pace in more than two years, suggesting a wave of new participants entering the market. However, the yardstick is an imperfect one, as existing users sometimes create multiple wallets. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y4mfyrjg
  • The publicly traded tech company MicroStrategy announced it would buy an additional $175 million of bitcoin, on top of a recent $250 million round that set a precedent for listed companies using the token as a cash management tool. CEO Michael Saylor explained the strategy in a new interview with CoinDesk's Danny Nelson. Saylor claims he could envision MicroStrategy holding its bitcoin "for 100 years." Link: https://tinyurl.com/y3tvt9le
  • The crypto exchange Kraken registered as a bank in Wyoming, which has passed crypto-friendly state regulations in hopes of becoming an industry hotbed. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y237xyn4
  • Blockchain Capital joined the Libra Association, a welcome development for the Facebook-led consortium. It suffered several departures of big-name partners earlier this year but is nevertheless pressing ahead with plans to launch a new stablecoin. Link: https://tinyurl.com/y5z5j5c8
